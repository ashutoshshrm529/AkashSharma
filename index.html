I am a student at Carnegie Mellon University pursuing a Master's degree in Robotics. As a member of the Robot Perception Lab, advised by Prof. Michael Kaess at the Robotics Institute, my research focuses on semantically assisted long term robotic mapping and 3D reconstruction through graph-based SLAM. More generally, I am interested in making robotic spatial understanding feasible.

Before joining CMU, I spent two years as a Software Developer at Infinera, where I developed infrastructure software for fault-tolerant optical communications, that manages long haul internet traffic. I received a Bachelor's degree in Engineering from Sri Jayachamarajendra College of Engineering (SJCE) in 2017, ranking amongst the top 10 students. As an undergraduate student, I have also taught and organized Robotics boot camps at SJCE for freshman students.

Object based dense SLAM for mobile robots
@ Robot Perception Lab / CMU
We introduce ObjectFusion, a scalable SLAM system that views the world as objects and planes. Leveraging the observation that most indoor environments are structured and occupied by recognizable objects, we present a sparse object graph mapping formulation, amenable to robot planning and dynamic scene tracking and understanding. Using a probabilistic data association method, and a novel way of orienting objects relative to detected planes, we show that this natural representation can enable drift free large scale indoor reconstruction, and enable lifelong SLAM in dynamic environments.
